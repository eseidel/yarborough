### **I. Overview & General Approach**

The Standard American Yellow Card (SAYC) was designed to be a simple, modern method allowing players in casual partnerships (like online play or partnership desks) to have a solid understanding without complex system discussions.

**Opening Guidelines**

- **5-Card Majors:** You normally open 5-card majors in all seats.
- **Minor Suits:**
  - Open **1♣** with 3-3 in the minors.
  - Open **1♦** with 4-4 in the minors.
  - _Elaboration:_ This is often called "Better Minor." With 3 diamonds and 3 clubs, you bid the club. With 4 diamonds and 3 clubs, you bid the diamond.
- **Equal Length:** Open the higher of long suits of equal length (5-5 or 6-6).
  - _Elaboration:_ This facilitates re-bidding. Opening the higher-ranking suit allows you to bid the lower-ranking suit on the next round, keeping the auction lower.

**Point Ranges for Balanced Hands**

- **1NT:** 15–17 HCP.
- **2NT:** 20–21 HCP.
- **3NT:** 25–27 HCP.
- **Note:** Notrump openings may include a 5-card major or minor if the hand is balanced.

### **II. 1NT Opening (15-17) & Responses**

A 1NT opening promises a balanced hand and 15-17 points.

#### **Responses to 1NT**

**1. Stayman (2♣)**

- **Meaning:** Non-forcing, asks for a 4-card major.
- **Opener’s Replies:**
  - **2♦:** No 4-card major.
  - **2♥:** 4 hearts (may also have 4 spades).
  - **2♠:** 4 spades (denies 4 hearts).
- _Elaboration:_ If opener has both majors, the standard SAYC agreement is to bid **2♥** first. If responder doesn't have hearts, they will bid 2NT or 3NT, and opener can then mention spades.

**2. Jacoby Transfers (2♦, 2♥)**

- **2♦:** Transfers to Hearts. Opener accepts by bidding **2♥**.
- **2♥:** Transfers to Spades. Opener accepts by bidding **2♠**.
- **After Acceptance:**
  - **Pass:** Content to play partscore.
  - **2NT / 3 of Major:** Invitational.
  - **New Suit:** Natural and **Game Forcing**.
  - **3NT:** Offers a choice between 3NT and 4 of the major.
- _Elaboration:_ Transfers allow the strong hand (opener) to become the declarer, hiding their cards from the defense.

**3. Minor Suit Responses**

- **2♠:** Forces opener to bid **3♣**. Responder can pass (club bust) or correct to **3♦** (diamond bust).
- **3♣ / 3♦:** Natural, showing a 6+ card suit and invitational values to 3NT.
- **Note:** To show slam interest with a minor, bid Stayman (2♣) first, then jump in the minor.

**4. Gerber (4♣)**

- **Meaning:** Asks for Aces. This applies over **any** 1NT or 2NT bid by partner.
- **Responses:**
  - **4♦:** 0 or 4 Aces.
  - **4♥:** 1 Ace.
  - **4♠:** 2 Aces.
  - **4NT:** 3 Aces.
  - **King Ask:** **5♣** asks for Kings (guarantees partnership holds all Aces).

**Interference over 1NT**

- **If Opponents Double:** System is "On" (2♣ is still Stayman, transfers apply).
- **If Opponents Bid:** System is "Off."
  - Bids are natural.
  - **Cuebid:** Acts as a Game Forcing Stayman substitute.

### **III. Major Suit Openings (1♥ / 1♠) & Responses**

**Opening:** Shows 5+ cards in the suit.
**Responses:**

- **1♠ (over 1♥):** 4+ spades, 6+ points. Tends to deny a heart fit.
- **1NT:** 6–9 points. **Non-Forcing**. Denies 3-card support or 4 spades.
- _Elaboration:_ In more advanced systems (2/1), 1NT is forcing. In SAYC, opener can Pass with a balanced minimum.
- **2 of Major (Raise):** 6–10 dummy points, 3+ card support.
- **2 of New Suit:** 10+ points, promises 4+ cards. Forcing for one round.
- **2NT (Jacoby 2NT):** Game-forcing raise (13+ dummy points). Asks opener to show a short suit (singleton/void) to evaluate slam.
- **3 of Major (Limit Raise):** 10–11 dummy points, 3+ card support.
- **Jump Shift (e.g., 1♥ - 3♦):** Strong, invites slam.

**Opener's Rebids:**

- **Minimum (13–15):** Rebid 1NT, raise partner, or rebid own suit at lowest level .
- **Medium (16–18):** Jump raise or jump rebid own suit.
- **Maximum (19+):** Jump Shift or Jump to Game.

### **IV. Minor Suit Openings (1♣ / 1♦) & Responses**

**Opening:**

- **1♦:** 3+ diamonds (usually 4 unless 4-4-3-2).
- **1♣:** 3+ clubs.
  **Responses:**
- **1 of Major:** 4+ cards, 6+ points.
- **No Forcing Minor Raise:** There is no forcing raise for minors in SAYC.
- **2NT:** 13–15 HCP, Game Forcing.
- **3NT:** 16–18 HCP.

---

### **V. Strong 2♣ Opening**

**Opening:** Shows 22+ points or playing equivalent.
**Responses:**

- **2♦ (Waiting):** Artificial, waiting for opener to describe hand.
- **2♥/2♠/3♣/3♦:** Natural, 5+ suit, 8+ points (Game Forcing).
- **2NT:** Balanced, 8 HCP.
- **Follow-up:** If opener rebids 2NT (showing 22–24 balanced), systems are on (Stayman, Transfers) .

---

### **VI. Preemptive Bidding**

**Weak Two Bids (2♦, 2♥, 2♠)**
**Requirements:** 5–11 HCP, good 6-card suit.
**Responses:**

- **2NT:** Forcing, asks for a "Feature" (Ace or King outside the trump suit).
- Opener rebids suit with minimum (5-8 pts).
- Opener bids feature suit with maximum.
- **New Suit:** Forcing one round (RONF - Raise Only Non-Force).
- **Raise:** Preemptive (to play).

**3 and 4 Level Openings**

- **Rule of 2, 3, 4:**
- **Vulnerable:** Be within 2 tricks of your bid.
- **Equal Vul:** Be within 3 tricks.
- **Not Vulnerable:** Be within 4 tricks.

### **VII. Competitive & Defensive Bidding**

**Overcalls**

- **Suit Overcall:** 8–16 points. 5+ card suit.
- **1NT Overcall:** 15–18 points, balanced (stopper in opponent's suit implied).
- **Note:** System is "Off" except **2♣** is Stayman.
- **Weak Jump Overcall:** Preemptive (same as opening weak 2).

**Takeout Double (X)**

- **Requirements:** Opening values, short in opponent's suit, support for unbid suits.
- **Response:** Partner _must_ bid unless they have a penalty pass hand. Jump response is invitational.

**Negative Double**

- **Usage:** Used through **2♠**.
- **Meaning:** Promises 4 cards in the unbid major(s).
- e.g., **1♣ - (1♦) - Dbl** shows 4 hearts and 4 spades.
- e.g., **1♦ - (1♠) - Dbl** shows 4 hearts and 6+ points.

**Michaels Cuebid**

- **Direct Cuebid (e.g., 1♣ - 2♣):** Shows a 5-5 two-suiter.
- If opening was a minor: Shows Majors.
- If opening was a major: Shows the _other_ major and an unspecified minor.

---

### **VIII. Slam Bidding & Leads**

**Blackwood (4NT)**

- **Meaning:** Asks for Aces.
- **Responses:**
  - **5♣:** 0 or 4 Aces.
  - **5♦:** 1 Ace.
  - **5♥:** 2 Aces.
  - **5♠:** 3 Aces.
  - **5NT:** Asks for Kings (guarantees all Aces held).

**Defensive Signals**

- **Leads:**
  - **Suits:** 4th best (e.g., K8**6**2).
  - **Notrump:** Top of interior sequence or 4th best.
  - **Touching Honors:** Lead the top honor (e.g., **K**QJ).
- **Discards/Signals:** High encourages, Low discourages.
